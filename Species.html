Introduction

Wildlife conservation is critical to preserving biodiversity. Below are examples of endangered species that face significant challenges due to habitat loss, poaching, and climate change.

Endangered Species

Amur Leopard

Estimated population: Fewer than 100 individuals in the wild.

Threats: Habitat destruction, poaching.

Indian Rhinoceros

Estimated population: Around 4,014 individuals.

Threats: Poaching for the illegal trade in rhino horn.

Vaquita

Estimated population: Fewer than 10 individuals.

Threats: Bycatch in illegal gillnets, habitat loss.
